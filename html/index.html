The Gran Torino is named after the city of Turin (Torino, in Italian), considered "the Italian Detroit". Produced by Ford between 1968 and 1976. Brougham 2-door Hardtop 351 V-8 Cruise-O-Matic It was featured in the starsky and hutch tv show and the movie "Gran Torino" by Clint Eastwood.

The vehicle of choice was Starsky's two-door Ford Gran Torino, which was bright red, with a large white vector stripe on both sides only 4 used in production. limited-production version of the Gran Torino hero is this iconic red-and-white 1976 Ford Gran Torino.
This car is a collectible with about 1,000 - 1,300 produced. This one has at least been restored with a fresher bright red paint job, iconic white stripe, chrome, mag wheels and magnetic bubble light for the roof.

Walt Kowalski (Clint Eastwood) is a retired auto worker and Korean War vet that lives his life alone and disregards the many Asian, Latino and black families in his neighborhood. He becomes a reluctant hero when he stands up to the local gang who tried to force an Asian teen to steal Walt's treasured car. Although this happens they form a unlikely friendship and he learns he has more in common with his neighbors than he thought.
